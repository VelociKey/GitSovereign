# K107: GTM Strategy - Project GitSovereign

GitSovereign employs a two-pronged GTM strategy targeting both developer sentiment and organizational compliance.

## Prong 1: Buzz/Solo (Bottom-Up)
- **Objective**: Drive widespread adoption and developer buzz through an ultra-fast, "cool" tool (Firehorse HUD).
- **Value Proposition**: "Zero-Disk" security (never touches my local drive) and sub-second local recovery.
- **Distribution**: GitHub Marketplace, developer community outreach, open-source workstation agent.
- **Success Metric**: Developer daily active users (DAU) and local archive volume.

## Prong 2: Value/Enterprise (Top-Down)
- **Objective**: Monetize organizational compliance and availability requirements.
- **Value Proposition**: Verifiable SOC2 evidence, identity-anchored access control, and organization-wide storage deduplication.
- **Distribution**: Direct sales to CTOs/CISOs, partnership with Google Cloud (via MCP/GCS integration), SOC2 compliance advisors.
- **Success Metric**: Value-Unit throughput (Verifications, Assurance Reports), ARR from compliance-tier subscriptions.

## The Bridge: Availability as an Asset
We move away from being a "commodity backup" by framing GitSovereign as an **Availability Asset**. We don't just "back up" data; we ensure its **Sovereignty** by providing a sub-second, human-verifiable recovery path that is independent of the source platform.
